Introduction to Gratuity

What is Gratuity?

Gratuity is a statutory retirement benefit paid by an employer to an employee as a token of appreciation for long and continuous service. It provides financial security to employees when they leave an organization after completing the required period of service. It is governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 for eligible establishments and employees.

Purpose of Gratuity
  • Reward employees for long-term service.
  • Provide financial assistance after separation.
  • Offer social security benefits.
  • Encourage employee retention.
  • Recognize loyalty and dedicated service.

Objectives of the Payment of Gratuity Act

The Act aims to provide a uniform legal framework for gratuity payments, ensuring that eligible employees receive financial benefits upon retirement, resignation, superannuation, death, or permanent disablement, subject to applicable legal provisions.

Applicability of the Payment of Gratuity Act

The Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 applies to establishments that meet the conditions prescribed under the Act. Once applicable, it generally continues to apply even if the number of employees later falls below the prescribed threshold, subject to applicable law.

  • ✔ Factories
  • ✔ Mines
  • ✔ Oilfields
  • ✔ Plantations
  • ✔ Ports
  • ✔ Railway Companies
  • ✔ Shops and Commercial Establishments covered under applicable State laws
  • ✔ Other establishments as notified by the Central Government

Eligibility for Gratuity

An employee generally becomes eligible for gratuity after completing the required period of continuous service under the Act. Certain situations, such as death or permanent disablement, have special provisions where the usual minimum service requirement does not apply.

Situation Eligibility
Retirement Generally payable if the employee satisfies the applicable continuous service requirement.
Resignation Generally payable if the applicable service requirement is met.
Superannuation Eligible subject to the Act.
Death Minimum service requirement is generally not applicable; payment is made to the nominee or legal heir.
Permanent Disablement Minimum service requirement is generally not applicable.

Continuous Service

Continuous service means uninterrupted service. Certain interruptions are treated as continuous service under the Act, such as:

  • ✔ Authorised leave
  • ✔ Sickness
  • ✔ Accident
  • ✔ Maternity leave (as applicable)
  • ✔ Lay-off
  • ✔ Lock-out
  • ✔ Strike that is not illegal
  • ✔ Cessation of work not caused by the employee

When is Gratuity Payable?

  • 👴 Retirement
  • 🏁 Superannuation
  • 📝 Resignation (subject to eligibility)
  • 💔 Death of an employee
  • ♿ Permanent disablement

Important Definitions

Term Meaning
Employee A person employed for wages in an establishment covered by the Act, subject to its provisions.
Employer The person or authority responsible for the management and control of the establishment.
Nominee A person nominated by the employee to receive gratuity in the event of the employee's death.
Superannuation Retirement on reaching the age specified in the terms of employment.
