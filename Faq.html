EPF & ESI - Frequently Asked Questions

FAQs for Employees, Employers and HR Professionals

1. What is EPF?

EPF is a retirement savings scheme where employees and employers contribute monthly, helping build financial security and long-term retirement benefits.

2. Who manages the EPF scheme?

The Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) administers EPF, maintains member accounts, collects contributions, and provides retirement-related services nationwide.

3. What is ESI?

ESI is a social security scheme providing eligible employees medical care, sickness, maternity, disablement, and dependent benefits through ESIC facilities.

4. Who administers the ESI scheme?

The Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) administers ESI by collecting contributions and providing healthcare and social security benefits.

5. What are the main benefits of EPF?

EPF provides retirement savings, interest earnings, pension benefits through EPS, account transfer, and withdrawals according to applicable legal provisions.

6. What benefits are available under ESI?

Eligible insured employees receive medical treatment, sickness benefits, maternity support, disablement compensation, and dependent benefits under prescribed conditions.

7. What is the Universal Account Number (UAN)?

UAN is a unique EPFO identification number enabling employees to manage, transfer, and monitor provident fund accounts across employers.

8. Do both employees and employers contribute?

Yes. Both employees and employers contribute to EPF and ESI according to applicable laws, contribution rates, and eligibility requirements.

9. Can an employee transfer their EPF account?

Yes. Employees can transfer their EPF balance between eligible employers using their Universal Account Number and EPFO online services.

10. Why are EPF and ESI important?

They provide financial security, retirement savings, healthcare benefits, and social protection, improving employee welfare and long-term economic stability.
