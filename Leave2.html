Payment of Bonus Act, 1965

The Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 provides for payment of bonus to eligible employees based on the profits or productivity of an establishment. It aims to ensure that employees receive a fair share of the organization's success.

Objectives

  • Share profits with employees.
  • Improve employee motivation and productivity.
  • Promote industrial harmony.
  • Provide statutory bonus to eligible employees.

Eligibility

Criteria Requirement
Minimum Service At least 30 working days in a financial year.
Salary Eligibility As notified by the Government from time to time.
Applicable Establishment Factories and eligible establishments covered under the Act.

Bonus Rates

Type Percentage
Minimum Bonus 8.33% of eligible wages
Maximum Bonus 20% of eligible wages

When Bonus is Paid

  • Usually after the financial year ends.
  • Generally before the statutory due date prescribed under the law.
  • Paid through bank transfer, cheque, or other approved modes.

Example Calculation

Monthly Eligible Salary : ₹18,000

Annual Salary = ₹18,000 × 12 = ₹2,16,000

If Bonus Rate = 10%

Bonus = ₹2,16,000 × 10% = ₹21,600

Important Points

  • Minimum statutory bonus is 8.33%.
  • Maximum bonus is 20%.
  • Bonus is payable even if the employer has not declared dividends, subject to the provisions of the Act.
  • Employees dismissed for fraud, theft, riotous or violent behaviour, or sabotage may be disqualified from bonus as per the Act.
  • Maintain bonus registers and payment records for compliance.
