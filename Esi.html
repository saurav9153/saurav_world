Employee Provident Fund (EPF) & Employee State Insurance (ESI)

Brief Guide for Employees, Employers and HR Professionals

Employee Provident Fund (EPF)

EPF is a retirement savings scheme managed by the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO). Both the employee and employer contribute every month to build a retirement corpus.

Key Highlights

  • Applicable to eligible establishments covered under the EPF law.
  • Employee contribution is generally 12% of Basic Salary + Dearness Allowance.
  • Employer also contributes, with a portion going to the Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS), subject to applicable rules.
  • Amount earns interest declared by EPFO.
  • Withdrawal and transfer are available as per EPF provisions.
  • UAN (Universal Account Number) helps manage PF accounts across jobs.
Particular Description
Purpose Retirement Savings
Managed By EPFO
Employee Contribution Generally 12%
Employer Contribution As prescribed under EPF/EPS rules
Benefit Retirement Corpus with Interest

Employee State Insurance (ESI)

ESI is a social security and health insurance scheme administered by the Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC). It provides medical care and certain cash benefits to eligible employees and their dependants.

Key Highlights

  • Applicable to eligible establishments covered under the ESI law.
  • Employee and employer contribute at rates notified by the Government.
  • Provides medical treatment through ESIC facilities.
  • Includes sickness, maternity, disablement and dependant benefits, subject to eligibility.
  • Supports employees during illness and employment-related injuries.
Particular Description
Purpose Medical & Social Security
Managed By ESIC
